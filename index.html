Training Principles
01
Full Range of Motion, Every Rep
Dead hang to chin over bar. Chest to floor on push-ups. Lock out at the bottom of every dip. Partial reps rob you of the stimulus. HFK's physique was built on honest reps, not inflated round counts.
02
Squeeze the Target Muscle
Mind-muscle connection isn't a gym bro myth — it's Hannibal's actual cue. On every push movement, squeeze your chest and triceps. On every pull, feel your lats engage. Core braced on everything.
03
Push/Pull in the Same Session
HFK trains chest and back together, never splits them across days. Antagonist pairing allows faster recovery between sets and higher total session volume — the engine of fat loss and muscle gain simultaneously.
04
Endurance Over Max Strength
The Around The World method is high-rep, high-volume, short rest. This builds dense muscle, elevates metabolism, and burns fat — without a barbell in sight. Volume is the variable. Keep adding rounds.
05
Master Basics for 8–12 Months First
Hannibal's exact advice: drill fundamental movements for 8–12 months before chasing advanced skills. Pull-ups, dips, and push-ups done with perfect form are more productive than sloppy muscle-ups. Earn the progressions.
06
Nutrition: Protein + Deficit
HFK currently eats lean protein, healthy fats, vegetables, and complex carbs — and recommends quality protein powder as a minimum. For fat loss, keep protein at ~0.8–1g per lb of bodyweight and maintain a modest caloric deficit. Let the training volume do the rest.
12-Month Progression
1
Months 1–3
Foundation — Around The World
Build the habit. Master pull-up lockout, dip depth, and push-up form. Work up to 8+ quality rounds in a 60-min session. No skills work yet — this is pure fundamentals. 3 sessions/week.
2
Months 4–6
Intensity — Add Variations
Introduce close-grip pull-ups, diamond push-ups, and slow-tempo dips. Extend sessions to 75–90 min. Add Saturday skill work (muscle-up negatives, L-sit holds). 4 sessions/week.
3
Months 7–9
Volume Peak — Max Rounds
Chase your personal records on Friday Volume Day. Incorporate jump push-ups, clapping pull-ups, and explosive dips for power. Begin tuck front lever holds. Nutrition locked in with consistent protein targets.
4
Months 10–12
Skills + Refinement
Muscle-ups in working sets. Straddle front lever attempts. Begin planche push-up progressions (feet elevated). This is the HFK level — achieved through 9 months of patient fundamentals, exactly as he prescribed.
Fat Loss Strategy
Caloric Deficit + High Volume = Recomp
High-rep calisthenics creates a significant metabolic demand. Pair 3–4 sessions/week with a 300–500 cal/day deficit and ~180g+ protein (assuming ~180 lb bodyweight). The volume preserves muscle while the deficit drives fat loss. Progress photos and weekly round counts are your best metrics — not the scale alone.
