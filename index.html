Exercise Progressions — Tom Peto Method
Merged Training Principles
01
AMRAP is Progressive Overload DRG
Without a barbell, rounds are your load. Every extra round in the same 15–20 min window is equivalent to adding weight. Track it weekly — progressive overload is how calisthenics builds muscle, not just fitness.
02
Full Range of Motion, Every Rep HFK
Dead hang to chin over bar. Chest to floor on push-ups. Lock out at the top and bottom of every movement. Half reps inflate your round count but kill your results. Honest reps only.
03
Master Progressions Before Moving Up Tom Peto
Tom Peto's system gives every movement three tiers: beginner → intermediate → advanced. Stay in the beginner tier until you can complete all reps with clean form. Chasing advanced variations too soon is the fastest way to plateau.
04
Push + Pull in the Same Week HFK
HFK considers splitting push and pull across different days "a waste of time." This program pairs Monday (push) and Wednesday (pull) to hit the full upper body twice per week — the minimum frequency for muscle growth.
05
Mobility is Not Optional Tom Peto
Wrist circles, kang squats, Cossack squats, and angel stretches aren't a warm-up formality. Tom Peto builds them into every session because calisthenics requires full joint range — especially wrists and shoulders. Skip them and the movements suffer.
06
Nutrition: Protein + Modest Deficit DRG
High-rep AMRAP circuits create a significant metabolic demand. Pair the training volume with ~0.8g protein per lb bodyweight and a 300–400 cal/day deficit. The volume preserves muscle while the deficit drives fat loss. Progress photos and round counts tell the real story.
